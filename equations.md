Problem: find $x \in R such that $x + 7 = 9$

Method 1:
$x + 7 = 9$

$\Rightarrow x + (7 - 7) = 9 - 7 \quad \because \text{x, 7, 9} 
\in R$ Transitivity holds. 

$\Rightarrow x + 0 = 2$

$\Rightarrow x = 2$


Method 2: 
$x + 7 = 9$

$\Rightarrow x + 7 - 9 = 9 - 9$

$\Rightarrow x + (-2) = 0$

$\Rightarrow x = 2 \because \text{2 and -2 are additive inverses to each other}$
